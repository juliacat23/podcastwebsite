Managing Your Mental Health in a Pandemic

Episode Transcript and Citations

Posted by Julia Catalano on March 30, 2021

Episode Transcript

Hello Everyone! Welcome back to another episode of "Not Your Average Podcast"! A podcast for college students. By college students. Today we will be exploring how to manage your mental health during the COVID-19 pandemic.

For those living in the United States, we recently passed the one-year anniversary of the COVID-19 lockdown. For most of us, we went from having in-person classes and eating lunches with our friends in the student union to having to move back home and take completely virtual courses in the blink of an eye. While these public health measures are absolutely essential in the fight against COVID-19, they adversely affect one's mental health. This is not an unknown or new phenomenon either!

Natural disasters, wars, and epidemics are known elicitors of poor mental health. In a study conducted by Jalloh et al., researchers discovered elevated rates of anxiety, depression, and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) symptoms in survivors of the 2014 - 2016 west Africa Ebola epidemic[1]. Those who lived in areas with a high concentration of Ebola cases displayed the highest prevalence of anxiety symptoms[1]. In a well-documented study by DiGiovanni et al., researchers identified a high prevalence of PTSD symptoms in those quarantined during the 2003 Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome, or SARS, outbreak in Canada[2]. Their research demonstrated the psychological stress imposed by public health quarantine orders[2]. Their findings were later collaborated by several publications emerging in early 2020 regarding the city-wide lockdown in Wahun, China[3]. Quarantine has an apparent adverse effect on one's mental health.

For those who have seen the Earth from space, and for the hundreds and perhaps thousands more who will, the experience most certainly changes your perspective. The things that we share in our world are far more valuable than those which divide us.
